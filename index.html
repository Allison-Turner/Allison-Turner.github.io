Peer-to-Peer Assisted Livestreaming Proof of Concept

6.829 Semester Project, Massachusetts Institute of Technology, Cambridge, MA

Code is viewable here and report is viewable here. Advised by Manya Ghobadi, PhD and Mohammed Alizadeh, PhD. Collaborated with Vishrant Tripathi and Guanzhou Hu.

Created a proof-of-concept system to prove the viability of peer-to-peer optimizations of livestreaming optimizations. Performed a literature search for current architectures and already attempted media peering schemes. Emulated a simple topology in Mininet, wrote a simplified livestreaming application with FFmpeg and MPlayer, wrote a peering-capable router with OpenFlow and using a POX controller, and viewed network traffic with Wireshark. Found demonstrable decreases in latency using peered connections to audience members.
